# Structural Patterns

This directory documents recurring structural patterns
that tend to produce inadmissible material.

Patterns do not classify cases.
They describe how structural failures manifest.

---

## Purpose

Structural patterns exist to:

- Identify recurring forms of instability
- Detect early warning signals
- Support consistent failure mode assignment
- Improve recognition across domains

Patterns operate at a higher level of abstraction
than individual cases.

---

## What patterns are not

Patterns are:

- Not classification decisions
- Not failure modes
- Not case summaries
- Not lessons learned
- Not corrective guidance

A pattern may describe how contradictions emerge,
how assumptions drift, or how structural instability accumulates —
but it does not determine admissibility on its own.

---

## Relationship to failure modes

- **Failure modes** explain why material is inadmissible.
- **Patterns** describe how such structural failures tend to form.

A pattern may correspond to multiple failure modes.
A failure mode may manifest through multiple patterns.

They are related but not interchangeable.

---

## Structure

Each pattern is documented in its own directory.

A pattern description may include:

- Structural description
- Typical manifestation
- Trigger characteristics
- Cross-references to failure modes
- Cross-references to cases

Patterns may evolve as recognition improves,
but must not retroactively alter past case decisions.

---

## Usage

Patterns are analytical tools.

They may be referenced in:

- `case.md` (contextual explanation)
- Failure mode documentation
- Analytical reviews

They must not override or replace classification gates.

---

## Stability

Patterns may be clarified and expanded,
but their abstraction level should remain consistent.

They describe structure —
not judgment, not correction, not rehabilitation.
