# Contradiction: Population-Level Evidence vs Individual-Level Recommendation

## Context
This contradiction arises in medical guideline reasoning and preventive health recommendations.

## Claim A
Population-level studies show a statistically significant reduction in outcome X.

## Scope of Claim A
- Level: Population
- Evidence type: Aggregate statistical analysis
- Uncertainty: Explicitly acknowledged

## Claim B
Individuals should adopt intervention Y to reduce their personal risk.

## Scope of Claim B
- Level: Individual
- Evidence type: Normative recommendation
- Uncertainty: Not specified

## Implicit Dependency
Claim B assumes that population-level effects translate reliably to individual-level outcomes.

## Incompatibility
Population-level statistical effects do not entail predictable individual-level benefit.
The dependency from Claim A to Claim B is not admissible without additional assumptions.

## STOP Condition
This contradiction triggers STOP due to illegitimate level transfer
(population → individual without admissible bridging conditions).

## Notes
- No claim is asserted false.
- The contradiction concerns scope compatibility only.
