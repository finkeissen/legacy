# Implicit Assumption Conflict

This pattern describes a structural contradiction
caused by incompatible implicit assumptions
that are never made explicit or reconciled.

The conflict is not stated.
It emerges from what the material silently presumes.

---

## Description

An **implicit assumption conflict** exists when:

- Multiple assumptions are required for the material to function
- These assumptions are mutually incompatible
- None of the assumptions are explicitly declared or examined

The material proceeds as if all assumptions could hold simultaneously.

---

## Typical structure

This pattern commonly appears when material:

- Mixes incompatible world models without acknowledgment
- Assumes different default states in different sections
- Relies on background conditions that cannot coexist
- Shifts foundational premises without signaling a transition
- Treats contextual assumptions as universal

Because the assumptions are implicit,
the contradiction often goes unnoticed until stress is applied.

---

## Indicators

Common indicators include:

- Arguments that work only if read selectively
- Conclusions that require different premises
  depending on where they are applied
- Rules that assume incompatible baseline conditions
- Explanations that silently change what is “given”

---

## What this pattern is not

This pattern does **not** describe:

- Explicitly stated alternative assumptions
- Scenario-based reasoning with clear boundaries
- Deliberate exploration of competing premises
- Known trade-offs acknowledged in the text

The defining feature is *implicit incompatibility*,
not mere plurality.

---

## Relationship to failure modes

Implicit assumption conflicts frequently contribute to:

- Atomic failure (unstable basic units)
- Epistemic failure (unstable justification)
- Contextual or institutional failure
  when assumptions embed hidden role or power structures

The pattern itself does not determine admissibility.

---

## Usage

This pattern may be referenced in:

- `case.md` to explain structural instability
- Failure mode documentation as a contributing mechanism
- Analytical reviews of legacy material

It must not be used as a standalone STOP criterion.

---

## Notes

Implicit assumption conflicts are particularly resistant
to correction, because making one assumption explicit
often invalidates others.

They tend to resurface even after partial rewrites
unless the underlying premises are fully surfaced and reconciled.
