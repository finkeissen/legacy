# System Closure ≠ Completeness

## Level
Structural (E2)

## Core Claim / Pattern
Closed systems define internal boundaries.

## Frequent Overinterpretation
Closure is treated as completeness or finality.

## Where the Interpretive Transfer Occurs
From boundary definition to claims of exhaustiveness.

## Structural Residue
Closure is a design choice, not an epistemic endpoint.

## Why the Misinterpretation Is Tempting
Closed forms feel finished.

## Useful Checks
- **Ordering vs. explanation:** Does the structure explain anything, or merely organize?
- **Counterfactual:** Would the claim survive a re-partitioning or re-layering?
- **Scope:** Is authority inferred from coherence alone?
