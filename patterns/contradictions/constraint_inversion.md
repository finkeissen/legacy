# Constraint Inversion

This pattern describes a structural contradiction
caused by the inversion of constraints and objectives.

Conditions that were meant to limit or bound a system
become treated as primary goals,
while original objectives are subordinated or distorted.

---

## Description

A **constraint inversion** exists when:

- Constraints are introduced to restrict or safeguard behavior
- Over time, those constraints become treated as objectives
- Original goals are adjusted or compromised to satisfy constraints
- The system optimizes for compliance rather than purpose

The contradiction arises because constraints are optimized
as if they were ends in themselves.

---

## Typical structure

This pattern commonly appears when material:

- Treats safety, compliance, or metrics as success criteria
- Optimizes proxies while losing the underlying objective
- Reframes limitations as defining features
- Preserves form while hollowing out function
- Enforces rules that undermine their original intent

The inversion is often gradual and implicit.

---

## Indicators

Common indicators include:

- Success defined primarily by adherence to constraints
- Goals rewritten to fit existing limitations
- Rules justified by their own existence
- Performance measured by proxy without validation
- Increasing rigidity alongside decreasing effectiveness

---

## What this pattern is not

This pattern does **not** describe:

- Legitimate prioritization of safety over performance
- Explicit trade-offs between competing objectives
- Deliberate constraint-driven design with stated intent
- Systems designed around a single overriding limitation

The defining feature is *unacknowledged role reversal*
between means and ends.

---

## Relationship to failure modes

Constraint inversion often contributes to:

- Institutional failure (misaligned incentives)
- Epistemic failure (invalid success criteria)
- Contextual failure (loss of original purpose)

It frequently co-occurs with role confusion
and scope collapse.

---

## Usage

This pattern may be referenced in:

- `case.md` to explain goal distortion
- Failure mode documentation as a contributing mechanism
- Reviews of legacy systems driven by proxy metrics

It does not constitute a STOP decision on its own.

---

## Notes

Constraint inversion is difficult to reverse,
because removing or relaxing constraints
is often perceived as regression or risk,
even when original objectives are no longer served.
