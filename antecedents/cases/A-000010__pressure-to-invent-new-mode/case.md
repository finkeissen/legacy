# Case Description

## Overview
The archived material combines strong analytical reasoning with
implicit authority and situational urgency.

It does not fail cleanly along a single obvious dimension.

## Context
The material was produced in a high-pressure setting where rapid
decisions were required. Its authority derived from timing,
confidence, and partial expertise rather than formal mandate.

## Inadmissibility rationale
At first inspection, the case appears to require a new failure mode
to capture its structure.

On closer analysis, the failure resolves into:
- epistemic instability (confidence exceeds justification), and
- institutional failure (authority exercised without mandate).

No new failure mode is required.

## Test condition
This case exists to confirm that the taxonomy resists category creep
under pressure and ambiguity.
