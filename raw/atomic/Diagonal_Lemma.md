# Diagonal Lemma

## Core Result
The diagonal lemma guarantees, for any sufficiently expressive formal system, the existence of sentences that refer to their own provability or unprovability.

## Frequent Overinterpretation
Self-reference is taken to imply paradox, semantic depth, or metaphysical circularity.

## Where the Interpretive Transfer Occurs
A syntactic construction is mistaken for a semantic or ontological phenomenon.

## Philosophical Residue
The lemma is a technical tool enabling formal self-reference, not a theory of meaning.

## Why the Misinterpretation Is Tempting
Natural language associations of self-reference invite metaphysical projection.
