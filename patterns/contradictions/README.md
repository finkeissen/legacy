# Contradiction Patterns

This directory documents recurring structural contradiction patterns.

A contradiction pattern describes how incompatible assumptions,
claims, constraints, or rules coexist within the same material
without being resolved or acknowledged.

These patterns do not classify material.
They describe a common form of structural instability.

---

## Purpose

Contradiction patterns exist to:

- Make structural contradictions explicit
- Support early detection of instability
- Explain recurring failure configurations across domains
- Assist consistent assignment of failure modes

They operate at an abstract level,
independent of specific content or context.

---

## Definition

A **structural contradiction** exists when:

- Two or more elements cannot be simultaneously true or valid
- The contradiction is not localized or trivially removable
- The material continues to operate as if no contradiction existed

The contradiction may be implicit, distributed,
or only visible under certain conditions.

---

## Typical manifestations

Contradiction patterns often appear as:

- Mutually exclusive assumptions treated as compatible
- Rules that negate each other under normal operation
- Definitions that collapse when applied consistently
- Constraints that cannot be jointly satisfied
- Requirements that invalidate their own premises

These manifestations may remain latent
until the material is stressed, extended, or operationalized.

---

## What contradiction patterns are not

Contradiction patterns are:

- Not simple disagreements
- Not unresolved questions
- Not trade-offs or tensions
- Not paradoxes used deliberately and explicitly
- Not temporary inconsistencies during exploration

A contradiction pattern implies structural instability,
not exploratory uncertainty.

---

## Relationship to failure modes

Contradiction patterns describe *how* instability forms.

They often underlie multiple failure modes, including:
- Atomic failure
- Epistemic failure
- Institutional failure

A single contradiction pattern may contribute
to multiple inadmissibility decisions.

---

## Usage

Contradiction patterns may be referenced in:

- Pattern descriptions
- Failure mode documentation
- Case descriptions (`case.md`)

They must not be used as standalone STOP criteria.

---

## Stability

Pattern descriptions may be refined or expanded.

They should not be redefined in ways
that retroactively alter prior case interpretations.

Patterns document structure — not judgment.
