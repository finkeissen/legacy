# Equivalence of Categories

## Core Result
Two categories are equivalent if there exist functors between them that preserve all categorical structure up to natural isomorphism.

## Frequent Overinterpretation
Equivalence is interpreted as identity: the claim that the two mathematical domains are “really the same thing”.

## Where the Interpretive Transfer Occurs
Structural equivalence is mistaken for ontological identity. The preservation of relationships is read as sameness of objects.

## Philosophical Residue
Category-theoretic equivalence concerns representational adequacy, not metaphysical reduction.

## Why the Misinterpretation Is Tempting
The power of invariant description invites reification of structure into essence.
