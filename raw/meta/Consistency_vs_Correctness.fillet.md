# Consistency ≠ Correctness

## Level
Structural (E2)

## Core Claim / Pattern
A system can be internally consistent without being correct or adequate.

## Frequent Overinterpretation
Internal coherence is treated as evidence of truth.

## Where the Interpretive Transfer Occurs
From logical or structural consistency to correctness about the world.

## Structural Residue
Consistency is a constraint on systems, not a guarantee of correctness.

## Why the Misinterpretation Is Tempting
Consistency feels like reliability.

## Useful Checks
- **Ordering vs. explanation:** Does the structure explain anything, or merely organize?
- **Counterfactual:** Would the claim survive a re-partitioning or re-layering?
- **Scope:** Is authority inferred from coherence alone?
