# Rice’s Theorem

## Core Result
Rice’s theorem states that every non-trivial semantic property of programs is undecidable.

## Frequent Overinterpretation
It is taken to show that program behavior or meaning is fundamentally unknowable.

## Where the Interpretive Transfer Occurs
Undecidability of uniform procedures is mistaken for impossibility of understanding.

## Philosophical Residue
The theorem limits algorithmic classification, not interpretation.

## Why the Misinterpretation Is Tempting
Semantic undecidability is easily conflated with semantic opacity.
