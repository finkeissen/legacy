# Law of Large Numbers

## Core Result
The law of large numbers states that, under suitable conditions, sample averages converge to expected values as the number of trials increases.

## Frequent Overinterpretation
Probabilistic convergence is taken to imply determinism or inevitability in individual cases.

## Where the Interpretive Transfer Occurs
Asymptotic statements about limits are projected onto finite sequences or single events.

## Philosophical Residue
The theorem concerns long-run behavior of sequences, not guarantees about particular outcomes.

## Why the Misinterpretation Is Tempting
Everyday intuitions about frequency blur the distinction between limits and instances.
