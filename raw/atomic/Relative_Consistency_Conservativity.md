# Relative Consistency and Conservativity

## Core Result
Relative consistency results show that if one theory is consistent, then another is consistent; conservativity results show that an extension does not prove new theorems of a certain kind in the base language.

## Frequent Overinterpretation
Relative consistency is taken as proof that the extended axioms describe real entities or that the extended theory is “true”. Conservativity is taken as endorsement or triviality.

## Where the Interpretive Transfer Occurs
Syntactic non-contradiction is converted into ontological existence or truth. Proof-theoretic containment is mistaken for semantic or metaphysical harmlessness.

## Philosophical Residue
These results compare formal strength and commitments within proof theory; they do not establish existence claims or metaphysical legitimacy.

## Why the Misinterpretation Is Tempting
“Consistency” and “no new consequences” sound like validation. The distinction between formal permission and ontological commitment is blurred.
