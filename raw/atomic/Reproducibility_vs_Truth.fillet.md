# Reproducibility ≠ Truth

## Core Claim / Pattern
Reproducibility shows stability under repetition.

## Frequent Overinterpretation
Stable results are treated as guarantees of truth.

## Where the Interpretive Transfer Occurs
From methodological robustness to correspondence truth.

## Philosophical Residue
Truth requires interpretation and theoretical context.

## Why the Misinterpretation Is Tempting
Replication builds confidence.

## Useful Checks
- **Bridge principle?** What additional assumption connects the formal or descriptive result to an ontological or normative claim?
- **Scope?** Does the statement concern methods, models, or measurements — or reality itself?
- **Counterfactual:** Would changing the framework invalidate the claim?
