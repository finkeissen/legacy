# Beth Definability Theorem

## Core Result
Beth’s theorem states that if a relation is implicitly definable in a theory (fixed by all models of the theory), then it is explicitly definable in the theory.

## Frequent Overinterpretation
Implicit definability is treated as ontological determination: as if the theory already contains the entity “in reality” even without a definition.

## Where the Interpretive Transfer Occurs
Model-theoretic invariance is converted into metaphysical existence or essence. A fact about definability is mistaken for a claim about what there is.

## Philosophical Residue
The theorem connects two technical notions of definability under a semantics; it does not settle disputes about realism or meaning.

## Why the Misinterpretation Is Tempting
“Implicitly there” sounds like “really there”. The gap between semantic invariance and ontological commitment is easy to miss.
