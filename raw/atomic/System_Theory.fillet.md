# System Theory — Fillet

## 1. Core Formal Structure
System theory (in the broad, cross-disciplinary sense) introduces **systems**, **boundaries**, **states**, **inputs/outputs**, and **feedback**. In its engineering lineage (cybernetics/control), it is commonly formalized via:
- state-space models,
- transfer functions,
- feedback loops,
- stability/controllability/observability criteria.

In its sociological lineage, “system” and “environment” are often treated as conceptual primitives, with “observation” and “distinction” functioning as operational notions.

## 2. Standard Interpretation
Systems are routinely read as **real entities** with objective boundaries and intrinsic purposes. “Feedback,” “self-organization,” and “autopoiesis” are treated as explanatory mechanisms that replace causal detail rather than summarize it.

## 3. Illicit Upgrades
- **Model boundary → ontological boundary**: treating a chosen system boundary as “what really exists.”
- **Description → explanation**: using “self-organization” or “emergence” as if it were a causal account rather than a label for macro-regularity.
- **Information/feedback → meaning/agency**: importing semantic or intentional content into signals or control variables without an explicit semantics layer.
- **Observer-relative distinctions → metaphysical relativism**: moving from “different descriptions carve differently” to “there is no fact of the matter.”

## 4. Failure Modes
- Reifying “the system” into an agent.
- Treating equilibrium or stability as a normative ideal.
- Using “complexity” as a substitute for operational definitions.
- Confusing measurement/observation constraints with ontological indeterminacy.

## 5. What Survives
- Systems as **modeling conveniences**: boundary choice as pragmatic and revisable.
- Feedback/stability as **formal properties** of specified dynamics.
- Multi-level description as legitimate, provided the mapping between levels is explicit.

## 6. How It Reconnects
- Connects to the Matrix as: **Model Family + Interface Constraints**.
- Requires explicit metadata: boundary choice, variables, dynamics, measurement model, and intended use (prediction/control/explanation).
- Guardrail: no step from “effective system model” to “the world is fundamentally systems.”
