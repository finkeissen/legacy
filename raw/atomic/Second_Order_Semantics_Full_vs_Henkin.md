# Second-Order Semantics: Full vs. Henkin

## Core Distinction
Under full semantics, second-order quantifiers range over all subsets/relations; under Henkin semantics, they range over a specified domain of subsets/relations, yielding a model theory closer to first-order logic.

## Frequent Overinterpretation
Full semantics is treated as the uniquely correct or “intended” meaning of second-order logic, and Henkin semantics as a mere technical compromise.

## Where the Interpretive Transfer Occurs
A choice of semantics is promoted into a metaphysical privilege claim. Stronger categoricity or expressive effects are taken as proof of unique ontological determination.

## Philosophical Residue
Different semantics serve different purposes. Neither by itself establishes what mathematical reality is; they specify different inferential and model-theoretic regimes.

## Why the Misinterpretation Is Tempting
“Full” sounds like “the real one”. The success of categoricity results invites reification of semantic strength into truth.
