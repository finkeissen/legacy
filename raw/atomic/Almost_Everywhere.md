# Almost Everywhere

## Core Result
A property holds almost everywhere if the set of exceptions has measure zero.

## Frequent Overinterpretation
Measure-zero exceptions are treated as negligible or impossible in practice.

## Where the Interpretive Transfer Occurs
Measure-theoretic notions are confused with empirical or epistemic significance.

## Philosophical Residue
Measure zero does not entail impossibility or irrelevance.

## Why the Misinterpretation Is Tempting
The phrase “almost everywhere” suggests practical certainty.
