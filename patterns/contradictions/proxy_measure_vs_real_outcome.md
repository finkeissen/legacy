# Contradiction: Proxy Measure Treated as the Target Outcome

## Context
Common in medicine, economics, and management metrics.

## Claim A
Intervention Y improves proxy measure P.

## Scope of Claim A
- Level: Measurement proxy
- Evidence type: Observed change in proxy
- Uncertainty: Usually quantifiable

## Claim B
Therefore, intervention Y improves the real target outcome T.

## Scope of Claim B
- Level: Real-world target outcome
- Evidence type: Inferred causation
- Uncertainty: Often unaddressed

## Implicit Dependency
Claim B assumes that proxy P is a valid, stable surrogate for target T.

## Incompatibility
Proxy improvement does not entail target improvement unless surrogate validity is established under admissible criteria.
Without that, the dependency is not admissible.

## STOP Condition
Surrogate substitution / proxy-to-target collapse

## Notes
- No claim is asserted false.
- The contradiction concerns surrogate validity assumptions.
