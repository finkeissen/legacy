# Temporal Failure Mode

This failure mode applies when material becomes inadmissible
due to time-related instability, decay, or misalignment.

The failure arises because assumptions, validity, or relevance
do not persist across time in the way the material requires.

---

## Definition

A **temporal failure** exists when material:

- Depends on conditions that change over time
- Assumes persistence without renewal or verification
- Becomes invalid as circumstances evolve
- Lacks mechanisms for time-bounded validity

The material may have been admissible at creation time,
but is no longer safe or meaningful to reuse.

---

## Typical triggers

This failure mode is commonly triggered when:

- Time-sensitive data is treated as timeless
- Assumptions expire without detection
- Environmental or system drift is ignored
- Validity windows are implicit or absent
- Material lacks revision or sunset mechanisms

---

## What this mode does *not* cover

Temporal failure does **not** apply when:

- Time dependence is explicit and enforced
- Material is versioned and actively maintained
- Changes are monitored and bounded
- Failures arise primarily from context or institution

Those cases may fall under other failure modes.

---

## Recognition heuristic

Ask:

- When was this material last valid?
- What assumptions depend on time or change?
- What would invalidate it as time passes?

If these questions lack clear answers,
this failure mode likely applies.

---

## Notes

Temporal failure is often gradual and silent.

Material may remain in use long after
its assumptions have decayed beyond recognition.
