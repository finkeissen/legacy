# Craig’s Interpolation Theorem

## Core Result
If a sentence A entails a sentence B in first-order logic, then there exists an interpolant C using only the vocabulary shared by A and B such that A entails C and C entails B.

## Frequent Overinterpretation
Interpolation is read as showing that logic provides semantic “explanations” or that shared vocabulary captures the true meaning mediating the entailment.

## Where the Interpretive Transfer Occurs
A syntactic/semantic property of consequence is inflated into a claim about explanation, understanding, or meaning. Formal mediators are mistaken for causal or conceptual mediators.

## Philosophical Residue
Interpolation concerns the structure of derivations and vocabularies, not what makes B true or why B follows in an explanatory sense.

## Why the Misinterpretation Is Tempting
The word “interpolant” suggests a hidden essence between A and B. Explanatory language is projected onto proof structure.
