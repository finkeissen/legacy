# Contradiction: Uncertainty Acknowledged, Action Presented as Settled

## Context
Common in risk communication and crisis narratives.

## Claim A
We do not know (or cannot measure) key parameters with sufficient precision.

## Scope of Claim A
- Level: Epistemic (knowledge state)
- Evidence type: Method limitation / uncertainty disclosure
- Uncertainty: Central

## Claim B
Therefore, the recommended action is clear, mandatory, or beyond dispute.

## Scope of Claim B
- Level: Practical prescription
- Evidence type: Asserted decisiveness
- Uncertainty: Suppressed

## Implicit Dependency
Claim B assumes that acknowledged uncertainty still authorizes a single determinate course of action.

## Incompatibility
Acknowledged epistemic uncertainty cannot simultaneously be used to justify decisive necessity
without an explicit admissible decision rule under uncertainty (and an explicit goal function).

## STOP Condition
Uncertainty collapse / implicit decision rule injection

## Notes
- No claim is asserted false.
- The contradiction documents a hidden decision rule.
