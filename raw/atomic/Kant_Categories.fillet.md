# Kant – Categories as Conditions of Knowledge

## Core Claim / Pattern
Kant’s categories (e.g., causality, substance) describe conditions for the possibility of experience and judgment, not properties of things-in-themselves.

## Frequent Overinterpretation
The categories are read as direct claims about the structure of reality itself.

## Where the Interpretive Transfer Occurs
From transcendental conditions of knowing to ontological claims about being.

## Philosophical Residue
A distinction between cognitive framework and world-structure; Kant provides a theory of objectivity-in-experience, not necessarily a metaphysics of things-in-themselves.

## Why the Misinterpretation Is Tempting
Because categories feel fundamental, it is tempting to treat them as building blocks of reality.

## Useful Checks
- **Bridge principle?** What additional assumption connects the formal/epistemic result to a claim about reality?
- **Scope?** Does the statement concern *models/concepts/methods* or *being itself*?
- **Counterfactual:** What would have to change for the result to stop holding — the world or the framework?
