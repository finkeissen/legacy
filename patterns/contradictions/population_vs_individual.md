# Contradiction: Population-Level Evidence vs Individual-Level Recommendation

## Context
Common in medicine, public health, and policy guidelines.

## Claim A
A population-level study shows a statistically significant reduction in outcome X under intervention Y.

## Scope of Claim A
- Level: Population (aggregate)
- Evidence type: Statistical association / average effect
- Uncertainty: Explicitly acknowledged (confidence intervals, heterogeneity)

## Claim B
An individual should adopt intervention Y to reduce their personal risk in a predictable way.

## Scope of Claim B
- Level: Individual
- Evidence type: Normative recommendation
- Uncertainty: Often unspecified or downplayed

## Implicit Dependency
Claim B assumes that the population-level average effect translates reliably to individual-level benefit.

## Incompatibility
Population-level averages do not entail predictable individual outcomes without additional admissible bridging assumptions
(e.g. subgroup stability, causal transportability, individual risk stratification).

## STOP Condition
Illegitimate level transfer (population → individual without admissible bridging conditions)

## Notes
- No claim is asserted false.
- The contradiction concerns scope compatibility only.
