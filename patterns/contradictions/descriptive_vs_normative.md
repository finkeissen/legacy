# Contradiction: Descriptive Statement Used as Normative Authorization

## Context
Common in ethics-adjacent policy arguments and expert recommendations.

## Claim A
The system behaves in way X (descriptive claim).

## Scope of Claim A
- Level: Descriptive / observational
- Evidence type: Empirical report
- Uncertainty: May be present

## Claim B
Therefore, we must do Y (normative prescription).

## Scope of Claim B
- Level: Normative / prescriptive
- Evidence type: Value-laden decision rule
- Uncertainty: Typically absent

## Implicit Dependency
Claim B assumes Claim A provides normative authority (an 'ought').

## Incompatibility
No normative force follows from a descriptive claim without an explicit admissible value premise.
If the value premise is hidden, the reasoning injects norms illegitimately.

## STOP Condition
Norm injection / is–ought slippage

## Notes
- No claim is asserted false.
- The contradiction documents an inadmissible dependency.
