# Authority Leakage

This pattern describes a structural contradiction caused by the leakage of
authority beyond its legitimate or intended boundaries.

Decisions, claims, or rules carry force they were not designed to have.

---

## Description

An **authority leakage** exists when:

- Authority is inferred rather than granted
- Informal influence substitutes for mandate
- Advisory material is treated as binding
- Experimental guidance becomes policy by drift

The contradiction arises because authority is exercised
without corresponding responsibility or accountability.

---

## Typical structure

This pattern commonly appears when material:

- Uses confident tone without mandate
- Is reused in contexts with higher stakes
- Lacks clear audience or decision boundary
- Migrates from internal to external use

---

## Indicators

Common indicators include:

- “Soft” guidance enforced as rule
- Decisions justified by precedent alone
- Responsibility gaps when outcomes fail
- Claims of authority without traceable source

---

## Relationship to failure modes

Authority leakage often contributes to:

- Institutional failure (illegitimate power)
- Role confusion (unclear decision rights)
- Epistemic failure (false authority)

---

## Notes

Authority leakage is difficult to reverse,
because removing assumed authority is often perceived
as undermining legitimacy.
