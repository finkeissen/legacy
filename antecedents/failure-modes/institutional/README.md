# Institutional Failure Mode

This failure mode applies when material is rendered inadmissible
by the institutional context in which it was produced, used, or governed.

The failure arises not from the content alone,
but from misalignment between roles, authority, incentives,
and structural responsibility.

---

## Definition

An **institutional failure** exists when material depends on,
assumes, or exploits institutional structures that are unstable,
misaligned, undefined, or no longer valid.

The material cannot be safely reused without recreating
the same institutional conditions that caused the failure.

---

## Typical triggers

This failure mode is commonly triggered when:

- Roles and responsibilities are ambiguous or conflicting
- Authority is assumed without clear mandate
- Decision rights are implicit or contested
- Incentives encourage distortion, suppression, or overreach
- Accountability mechanisms are absent or ineffective
- Institutional context has shifted, but material has not

---

## What this mode does *not* cover

Institutional failure does **not** apply when:

- The issue is purely technical or logical
- Failures occur at the level of individual mistakes only
- The material remains valid independent of organizational context
- The problem can be resolved without institutional change

Those cases may fall under other failure modes.

---

## Recognition heuristic

Ask:

- Would this material still function correctly
  if produced or used in a different institutional setting?
- Does its validity rely on specific roles, power structures,
  or governance arrangements?

If the answer is no,
this failure mode likely applies.

---

## Notes

Institutional failure is often invisible from within the institution.

Material may appear sound internally
while being structurally dependent on
fragile or unexamined institutional arrangements.
