# Atomic Failure Mode

This failure mode applies when material is structurally unsound
at the level of its smallest units.

The failure occurs locally, but its effects propagate.

---

## Definition

An **atomic failure** exists when individual components
(statements, assumptions, rules, definitions, or constraints)
are internally unstable, contradictory, or undefined
in a way that invalidates the material as a whole.

The material does not fail because of aggregation or interaction,
but because its basic elements are not reliable.

---

## Typical triggers

This failure mode is commonly triggered when:

- Core terms are used inconsistently or without stable definition
- Fundamental assumptions shift within the same material
- Base rules contradict each other
- Primitive constraints are underspecified or incompatible
- Assertions depend on implicit exceptions at the atomic level

---

## What this mode does *not* cover

Atomic failure does **not** apply when:

- Individual components are sound, but fail in combination
- The issue arises only at scale or through interaction
- The problem is primarily contextual or institutional
- The material could be stabilized by reordering or reframing

Those cases may fall under other failure modes.

---

## Recognition heuristic

Ask:

- If this material were reduced to its smallest meaningful units,
  would those units still be coherent and stable?
- Do basic statements hold independently of surrounding structure?

If the answer is no,
this failure mode likely applies.

---

## Notes

Atomic failure is often an early indicator.

Material exhibiting this mode tends to produce
secondary failures if extended, combined, or operationalized.
