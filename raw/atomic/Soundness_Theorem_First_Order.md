# Soundness Theorem (First-Order Logic)

## Core Result
A proof system is sound if every sentence provable in the system is valid in all models (i.e., derivability implies semantic consequence).

## Frequent Overinterpretation
Soundness is taken to mean that whatever is provable is “true in reality” in an unrestricted, model-independent sense.

## Where the Interpretive Transfer Occurs
A guarantee about validity relative to a fixed semantics is inflated into a metaphysical guarantee about truth. Proof-theoretic reliability is mistaken for ontological authority.

## Philosophical Residue
Soundness secures the correctness of inference relative to a semantics; it does not choose a privileged interpretation or settle what “truth” ultimately is.

## Why the Misinterpretation Is Tempting
“Sound” suggests epistemic certainty. The everyday meaning of truth is projected onto model-theoretic validity.
